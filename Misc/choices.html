Technology Choices (Hackers)

In general, the term "hacker" has been used in the public media, it generally refers to cyber criminals,
however a hacker may be anybody who uses their understanding of computer and network to decompose
and circumvent security protocols on a system, phone, or network etc... regardless of their motives.

Types of Hackers

Colours are used as general categories to describe the wide range of hackers.

-White (Positive)
 A white hat is a person who is hired or recruited to carry out an assault with express authority and well defined parameters.
White hats' job is to uncover, analyze, and evaluate vulnerabilities, exploits, and infections in specific targets.
The results of these expert engagements are immediately communicated to the target, allowing them to close any gaps and
improve their security status. White hats also were involved in the development of security methods and services on occasion.

-Black (Negative)
 Black hats, on the other hand, do significant purposeful harm and wealth at the expense of its targets.
Through destructive, unlawful internet activity, they are responsible for directing attack patterns and
conversely driving employment demand in the white-hat sector. What drives this eclectic collection of
black hats? Money, personal satisfaction, retribution, media attention, promotion of their ideas,
the quest of important data, and simply sheer fun are among the many motives and purposes that hackers
are drawn to.

-Grey (Neutral)
 Gray hats, as their name implies, have a more unclear meaning. Depending on your point of view, their
work may be considered as good or bad. The phrase "grey hat" is occasionally used to refer to those who
disobey the law without intending to commit a crime. Cyber vandals who vandalise websites and rogue
security researchers who publicly reveal identified vulnerabilities without alerting or obtaining
authorisation from their targets may be included in this description.

Hacking Ethics

-Ethics of a hacker can be one of the benchmarks to tell if a hacker is good or bad.
(What their motivation, permission and legality is)

-An ethical hacker can assist a company in determining where the most important data is kept and how
the best way to secure it. This process secures the collected data.

-Collaboration with a company to help reduce the risk of security breaches from black hat hackers.
This proves that this is work of a white hat hacker.

-White hats are frequently called upon to imitate or recreate the attacks from the black hats.
Ethical hackers must have their attention on the evolving technologies, weaknesses, dangers, and
attackers in order to do this. Although with the best efforts, staying ahead of the game and
protecting against advances from complicated black-hat networks is an ongoing struggle for security
experts.

-Many cybersecurity experts, researchers, and police agencies want to keep an eye on shady internet
hacking communities in order to learn about the latest attack patterns, tools, and danger environment.
Regardless of the advantages of learning through direct contacts to the dark side, white hats must be
aware of the delicate legal and ethical boundaries that exist in their job.
